# Debt Management Strategies

## Understanding Debt Types
- **High-interest debt** (credit cards, personal loans >15%): Should be the top priority for repayment.
- **Medium-interest debt** (car loans, equipment financing 5-15%): Important but less urgent.
- **Low-interest debt** (family loans, education <5%): Can be paid at a slower pace.

## The Three Payment Strategies

### 1. Avalanche Method
Pay off debts from **highest interest rate to lowest**. This mathematically minimizes the total interest you pay over time. Best for people who are disciplined and motivated by saving money.

### 2. Snowball Method
Pay off debts from **smallest balance to largest**. You eliminate individual debts faster, which creates a psychological sense of achievement. Best for people who need motivational wins to stay on track.

### 3. Hybrid Method (Recommended)
Address **urgent debts first** (those closest to due date or with penalties), then switch to the avalanche approach. This balances urgency with financial optimization.

## Key Principles
- Always pay at least the **minimum payment** on every debt to avoid penalties and credit damage.
- Direct any **extra income** (bonuses, large projects) toward your priority debt.
- Once a debt is paid off, redirect its payment toward the next debt (this is the "snowball" or "avalanche" effect).
- Avoid taking on **new debt** while paying off existing obligations.

## Debt-to-Income Ratio
Your total monthly debt payments should ideally be below **30% of your income**. If it exceeds 40%, this is a warning sign that requires immediate action, such as negotiating lower payments or consolidating debts.

## Tips for Freelancers
- Irregular income makes debt management harder. Use your **virtual salary** (from cashflow smoothing) to budget for debt payments.
- Build a **1-month buffer** before aggressively paying off debt, so a slow month doesn't cause missed payments.
- Communicate with lenders about your freelance income pattern — many will work with you on flexible due dates.
