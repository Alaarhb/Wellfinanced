# Egyptian Financial Context

## Common Financial Products in Egypt

### Savings Certificates (شهادات الادخار)
- Offered by major banks (National Bank of Egypt, Banque Misr, CIB, etc.).
- Fixed interest rates over 1-3 year periods.
- Generally considered low-risk and are popular savings instruments.
- Interest can be paid monthly, quarterly, or at maturity.
- Useful for building an emergency fund or saving for specific goals.

### Installment Plans (التقسيط)
- Very common in Egypt for electronics, furniture, and appliances.
- Usually offered through credit cards or store financing.
- Watch out for hidden fees and high interest rates on installment plans.
- Before taking an installment, calculate the total cost including interest and compare with paying cash.

### Credit Cards
- Interest rates in Egypt range from 1% to 25%+ annually.
- Minimum payments are typically 5-10% of the outstanding balance.
- Paying only the minimum leads to massive interest accumulation.
- Always try to pay the full balance each month.

## Currency and Inflation
- Inflation affects purchasing power. When budgeting, account for annual price increases of 10-30% on essentials.
- Freelancers earning in foreign currency (USD, EUR) have a natural hedge against local inflation.
- Keep some savings in stable currencies if possible.

## Financial Planning Tips for Egypt
1. **Build an emergency fund** equal to 3-6 months of expenses before investing.
2. **Avoid unnecessary installments** — the total cost is always higher than cash.
3. **Track the pound exchange rate** if you earn in foreign currencies.
4. **Automate savings** through standing orders to a separate savings account.
5. **Compare bank products** — interest rates and fees vary significantly between banks.

## Freelancing in Egypt
- The gig economy is growing rapidly in Egypt.
- Popular platforms: Mostaql (Arabic), Khamsat (Arabic), Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer.
- Payment methods: bank transfer, PayPal, Payoneer, Vodafone Cash.
- Consider opening a dedicated business bank account to separate personal and business finances.
