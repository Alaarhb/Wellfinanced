# Making Smart Purchase Decisions

## The Affordability Framework
Before any significant purchase, answer these questions:
1. **Can I pay cash without affecting my emergency fund?** → Buy it.
2. **Do I need to use installments?** → Apply the installment affordability test.
3. **Will this purchase earn me more money?** → It's an investment, not just a cost.
4. **Can I wait 30 days?** → If yes, it might be an impulse buy.

## How to Calculate If You Can Afford Something
### Quick Test
- **Monthly surplus** = Virtual Salary − All Expenses − Debt Payments
- If the item costs **less than your monthly surplus**: You can buy it this month
- If the item costs **1-3× your monthly surplus**: Save for 1-3 months first
- If the item costs **more than 3× your monthly surplus**: Use installments or a savings plan

### Detailed Analysis
1. Check your **current account balance**
2. Subtract your **minimum emergency fund** (2 months of expenses)
3. Subtract any **upcoming large expenses** in the next 30 days
4. The remaining amount is your **available purchasing power**
5. If the item costs more than this, it requires planning

## Needs vs Wants Decision Matrix
| Factor | Need (Essential) | Want (Discretionary) |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Impact on work/income | High | Low |
| Can you function without it? | No | Yes |
| Is there a cheaper alternative? | Check first | Usually yes |
| Urgency | Immediate | Can wait |
| Example | Work laptop, internet, transportation | Gaming console, designer clothes |

## Work Equipment Purchases (Freelancer-Specific)
Work tools are often investments, not just expenses:
- **Laptop**: If your current one slows you down, upgrading can increase productivity by 20-30%
- **Software subscriptions**: Tools that save you 5+ hours/month are worth it
- **Courses/certifications**: Calculate ROI — will this help you charge more or get more clients?
- **Home office setup**: Ergonomic chair and desk reduce health costs long-term

## Purchase Timing Strategies
- **Electronics**: Buy during sales (Black Friday, 11.11, Back to School)
- **Annual subscriptions**: Pay annually if cheaper than monthly (usually 20-40% savings)
- **High-income months**: Make large purchases after a good project, not during slow months
- **Price tracking**: Watch prices for 2-4 weeks before buying — prices fluctuate in Egypt

## Red Flags — Don't Buy If:
- You'd need to skip a debt payment to afford it
- Your emergency fund is below 1 month of expenses
- You're in a slow income period with no projects lined up
- You'd need to take a high-interest loan or credit card advance
- You already bought a similar item in the last 6 months
- The total installment cost is more than 20% above the cash price
