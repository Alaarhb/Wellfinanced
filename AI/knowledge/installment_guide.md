# Installment Affordability Guide

## Understanding Installments (التقسيط)
Installments (installments/BNPL) are a common way to purchase high-value items in Egypt. Before committing to any installment plan, you must analyze your financial capacity.

## The Affordability Rule
An installment is affordable if:
1. Your **monthly surplus** (income minus all expenses and existing obligations) is at least **1.5× the installment amount**.
2. Total debt-to-income ratio stays **below 35%** after adding the new installment.
3. You still have money going to **savings** (at least 10% of income).

## How to Calculate Installment Impact
1. **Current surplus** = Virtual Salary − Total Expenses − Existing Debt Payments
2. **New surplus** = Current Surplus − New Installment Amount
3. **New debt ratio** = (Existing Debt + New Installment) / Monthly Income × 100
4. If new surplus < 0 or new debt ratio > 35%, the installment is **NOT affordable**.

## Total Cost Analysis
Always compare the total cost of installment vs cash:
- **Total installment cost** = Monthly payment × Number of months
- **Cash price** = Listed price
- **Interest premium** = Total installment cost − Cash price
- If the interest premium exceeds 15% of the cash price, consider saving and paying cash.

## Installment Types in Egypt
- **0% Interest installments**: Offered by banks on credit cards for specific stores. Best option if available.
- **Bank installments**: Through credit cards with 1-2% monthly interest. Calculate total cost carefully.
- **Store financing**: Through companies like Valu, Contact, or store-specific plans. Often higher rates.
- **BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later)**: Short-term (3-6 months), usually 0% but watch for late fees.

## When to Say No
- Your emergency fund is below 2 months of expenses
- You already have more than 2 active installments
- The item is a want, not a need, and your savings goals are behind
- Your income is in a "struggling" or declining phase
- Adding this installment would push debt ratio above 35%

## Smart Installment Strategy
1. Never take an installment for a depreciating asset unless essential for work (e.g., laptop for freelancing)
2. Choose the shortest term you can afford to minimize total interest
3. Make extra payments when you have surplus months to pay off faster
4. Set up automatic payment to avoid late fees and penalties
