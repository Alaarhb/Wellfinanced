# Budgeting Basics for Freelancers

## The 50/30/20 Rule
The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting framework:
- **50% Needs**: Essential expenses like rent, utilities, food, transportation, and medical costs.
- **30% Wants**: Non-essential spending like entertainment, dining out, subscriptions, and shopping.
- **20% Savings & Debt**: Emergency fund, savings goals, and debt repayment.

For freelancers with irregular income, consider adjusting to 60/20/20 to ensure essentials are always covered.

## Emergency Fund
Every freelancer should build an emergency fund covering **3-6 months of essential expenses**. This protects against months with low or zero income. Start by saving even small amounts consistently.

## Virtual Salary Method
Instead of spending income as it arrives, freelancers should:
1. Calculate average monthly income over the last 6-12 months.
2. Set a "virtual salary" at 65-80% of the average (depending on income stability).
3. Transfer the virtual salary to a spending account each month.
4. Keep surplus in a buffer/savings account.

## Tracking Income Sources
Diversifying income across multiple platforms (Upwork, Mostaql, Khamsat, direct clients) reduces risk. If one source dries up, others can compensate. Aim for no single client making up more than 30% of total income.

## Seasonal Planning
Many freelancers experience seasonal income fluctuations. Identify your high and low seasons, save extra during peak months, and reduce spending during slow periods.
